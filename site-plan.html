Overview

Purpose

The purpose for this website is to let people know who we are and what we do. We want to give people and clients a quick and comprehensive guide on what our company offers. Not only will we explain our business model but we will provide customer feedback on the site. The site will also have pictures and stories from clients who have participated in our business. We will explain our different packages that we offer and provide a forum to receive information regarding pricing and scheduling. The site will also include an email opt-in forum.

Audience

Our target audience will be people who are looking to make a change in their lives. This could be recovering alcoholics, people who are looking to lose weight, or someone who just wants some change in their life. We will be open to all ages. However, ages 12-15 will require adult accompaniment and ages 16-18 will require parent permission. Our business is relatively pricey but we will offer reduced rates and even free in some situations. Every member of our target audience will have slightly different needs. However, we expect to be able to cater to the general population simply but providing the target audience with a life changing and exciting adventure. Our site will be accessible through desktop, laptop, or mobile technology. We are not yet accessible on gaming consoles or smart TV devices. The site will include clickable objects that the users will be able to interact with.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
